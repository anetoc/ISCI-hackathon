# T-CTRL, powered by ISCI — project summary

Genome-scale Perturb-seq shows which genes *change* T-cell state, but not which genes *control* it. We asked whether controllership is separable from raw effect size in the Marson CD4+ T-cell screen. Testing an index *against* effect magnitude failed: known regulators simply have ~99× larger effects, so magnitude wins by construction. Reframing the question — does a feature add signal *conditional on* magnitude? — revealed a magnitude-independent signal: regulators produce effects that are more **state-specific** and more **reproducible across donors**. The authoritative pre-specified M→M+C test gains +0.357 AUPRC (0.539→0.896; 95% CI [+0.117,+0.538]); after every fitting and negative-selection step is repeated out of fold, the conservative estimate remains +0.215 ([+0.074,+0.560], permutation p=0.010). The simpler ranking view is AUPRC 0.415→0.722. The signal replicates across three culture conditions, but a fully independent functional-regulator set fails, bounding the claim to canonical axis-defining regulators. As a sanity check it recovers IRF1, STAT6, GATA3 and SETDB1 near the top; as discovery it nominates experimental candidates such as IKBKB, ZC3H12A, RCOR1, BCLAF1 and PRKDC. The broader lesson is that in-dataset controllability benchmarks are magnitude-confounded and require a conditional or external test.
